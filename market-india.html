Market Overview

India: A Generational Opportunity for Canadian Business

India has emerged as one of the most significant economic stories of the 21st century. With a $3.7 trillion GDP, a rapidly expanding middle class of over 400 million people, and one of the world's fastest-growing major economies, India represents a generational commercial opportunity for Canadian businesses.

Canada's relationship with India is uniquely positioned: a large and influential Indian diaspora community in Canada, Commonwealth ties, and bilateral diplomatic relationships create a foundation that few other countries can match.

What CGEC adds is decisive: EICBI's 13-year, ground-level network of relationships with Indian government bodies, business chambers, regional governments, and institutional leaders across India's major cities. This is not a database — it is a relationship network built over more than a decade of sustained engagement through parliamentary summits, business forums, and the Europe India Journal.

The EICBI Advantage

Why EICBI's India Network is Irreplaceable

India is a market where relationships are everything. EICBI has been building them for 13 years.

01

Parliamentary-Level Relationships

Market Geography

Key Cities for Canadian Business in India

India is not a single market — it is a continent of markets. EICBI's network covers India's most commercially significant centres.

Mumbai

Financial Capital

India's financial and commercial hub. Home to the Bombay Stock Exchange, major banking institutions, and the headquarters of India's largest conglomerates. The primary entry point for Canadian financial services, professional services, and consumer goods.

New Delhi

National Capital & Government

India's political capital and home to all central government ministries, regulatory bodies, and diplomatic missions. Essential for any Canadian business requiring government relations, regulatory approvals, or public sector engagement.

Bengaluru

Technology & Innovation Hub

India's Silicon Valley — home to over 6,000 technology companies and the world's third-largest startup ecosystem. The destination for Canadian technology companies, AI firms, and innovation-sector businesses.

Hyderabad

Life Sciences & Technology

India's pharmaceutical capital and a rapidly growing technology hub. Significant opportunity for Canadian life sciences, biotech, and advanced manufacturing companies.

Chennai

Manufacturing & Automotive

India's automotive and manufacturing hub, with growing cleantech and advanced manufacturing sectors. Strong alignment with Canadian advanced manufacturing and clean technology capabilities.

Ahmedabad

Business & Agri-food

Gujarat's commercial capital — a rapidly growing business centre with strong agri-food, textile, and chemicals industries, and one of India's most business-friendly regulatory environments.

Where Canadian Businesses Excel in India

Six sectors where CGEC identifies the strongest alignment between Canadian capability and Indian market demand.

💻

Technology & AI

India's digital transformation — from UPI payments to smart cities — creates extraordinary demand for Canadian technology expertise, particularly in AI, cybersecurity, and enterprise software.

⚡

Clean Energy

India's commitment to 500GW of renewable energy by 2030 creates significant demand for Canadian cleantech — solar, wind, green hydrogen, and grid management technology.

🌾

Agri-food & Food Security

India's food processing sector is among the world's largest. Canadian agri-food technology, precision agriculture, and food safety expertise align well with India's food security priorities.

🏥

Life Sciences & Health

India's generic pharmaceutical industry and rapidly growing private healthcare sector create partnership opportunities for Canadian life sciences and health technology companies.

🎓

Education & EdTech

India produces the world's largest number of STEM graduates annually. Canadian universities and EdTech companies find strong partnership demand from Indian institutions and students.

⛏️

Mining & Critical Minerals

Canada and India's complementary mineral resources and processing capabilities create strong bilateral partnership opportunities in critical minerals for the energy transition.

Canada-India Connection

A Diaspora Advantage No Other Country Can Match

Canada is home to one of the world's largest and most influential Indian diaspora communities — a strategic asset that gives Canadian businesses a built-in network of cultural intelligence, business contacts, and market knowledge in India.

The Indian-Canadian community includes business leaders, academics, politicians, and professionals with deep ties to India's government, industry, and civil society. For Canadian businesses, this represents an invaluable source of market insight and introductions.

CGEC's programmes are designed to help Canadian businesses activate this diaspora advantage strategically — complemented by EICBI's 13-year institutional network across India.
