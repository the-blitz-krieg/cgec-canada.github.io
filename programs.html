Core Programmes

What CGEC Delivers

🗺️

Market Entry Assessment

Foundation Programme

Before entering any international market, Canadian businesses need an honest, expert assessment of their readiness, competitive positioning, and optimal entry pathway. CGEC's Market Entry Assessment is the starting point for all CGEC members.

Conducted by advisors with direct experience in the UK, EEA, and Indian markets, this assessment gives you a clear roadmap — not generic advice, but market-specific intelligence grounded in EICBI's 13 years of corridor knowledge.

  • Export readiness evaluation
  • Target market prioritisation
  • Regulatory landscape overview
  • Competitive positioning analysis
  • Entry structure recommendations
  • Partnership strategy framework
Apply for Assessment
✈️

Trade Missions

Flagship Programme

CGEC organises structured trade missions to London, Brussels, and key Indian cities — curated delegations where Canadian businesses meet pre-qualified partners, government officials, and institutional contacts through EICBI's network.

These are not tourism. Every meeting is pre-arranged, every delegate is briefed, and every mission has clear commercial objectives. Delegates consistently cite CGEC trade missions as the single most valuable investment in their international expansion.

  • Pre-qualified B2B meeting schedules
  • Government and institutional introductions
  • EICBI parliamentary network access
  • Sector-specific roundtables
  • Post-mission follow-up support
  • Delegation briefing materials
Register for Trade Mission
🤝

Partner Matchmaking

Network Programme

The right partner in a foreign market can make the difference between success and failure. CGEC's matchmaking programme draws on EICBI's verified network of distributors, investors, legal and regulatory advisors, and institutional contacts across all three corridors.

We don't send lists of contacts — we make introductions. There is a significant difference, and it is one of the core advantages of operating through a council with 13 years of institutional relationships.

  • Distributor and agent identification
  • Investment partner introductions
  • Legal and regulatory advisor referrals
  • Joint venture partner sourcing
  • Government body introductions
  • Trade association connections
Request Matchmaking
📋

Regulatory & Compliance Advisory

Advisory Programme

Navigating the regulatory landscape of foreign markets is one of the greatest barriers to Canadian SME exports. CGEC provides expert guidance on CETA provisions, UK market access post-Brexit, EU regulatory frameworks, and Indian FDI and business registration requirements.

We work with a network of specialist legal and regulatory advisors in each market — ensuring you receive advice that is current, market-specific, and actionable.

  • CETA provisions and maximisation
  • UK market access post-Brexit
  • EU regulatory framework navigation
  • Indian FDI regulations and structures
  • Business registration in target markets
  • Import/export compliance documentation
Request Advisory Session
🏛️

Government Relations

Institutional Programme

For many international business opportunities — particularly in regulated sectors, infrastructure, or public procurement — relationships with government bodies are essential. CGEC's government relations programme leverages EICBI's parliamentary-level network to facilitate introductions that would otherwise be inaccessible.

This is the programme where EICBI's European Parliament accreditation and 13 years of Track 1.5 diplomatic engagement delivers its most distinctive value for Canadian businesses.

  • European Parliament institutional introductions
  • UK government and parliamentary introductions
  • Indian federal and state government contacts
  • Trade promotion agency connections
  • Investment authority introductions
  • Public procurement guidance and support
Enquire About Government Relations
📊

Market Intelligence Briefs

Intelligence Programme

CGEC members receive quarterly intelligence reports on trade policy developments, sector opportunities, and regulatory changes across UK, EEA, and Indian markets — co-produced with EICBI's policy and research team.

These are not generic trade publications. They are decision-making tools: specific, current, and focused on what matters for Canadian businesses operating in or preparing to enter these markets.

  • Quarterly market intelligence reports
  • Regulatory change alerts
  • Sector opportunity briefings
  • Trade policy analysis
  • EICBI policy publications and journals
  • Annual market outlook report
