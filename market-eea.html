Market Overview

The EEA: Canada's Most Structured Export Opportunity

The Comprehensive Economic and Trade Agreement (CETA) between Canada and the EU eliminates tariffs on 98% of product categories and opens European government procurement markets to Canadian suppliers.

Yet most Canadian SMEs are not fully leveraging CETA. CGEC's EEA market programme helps Canadian businesses understand, navigate, and maximise the opportunities CETA creates across 27+ EU member states.

Crucially, CGEC's exclusive partnership with EICBI adds a dimension no trade agreement can provide: direct institutional access to the European Parliament and EU Commission officials.

CETA Framework

What CETA Delivers for Canadian Business

CETA is one of the most ambitious bilateral trade agreements ever negotiated. Here is what it means for Canadian exporters in practice.

📦

Tariff Elimination

98% of tariff lines eliminated immediately. Virtually all Canadian goods can enter EEA markets duty-free or at reduced rates.

🏛️

Procurement Access

Canadian businesses gain access to European government procurement markets — worth hundreds of billions annually and rarely explored by Canadian SMEs.

👔

Professional Services

Mutual recognition of professional qualifications, facilitating movement of Canadian professionals and service firms into European markets.

💡

Intellectual Property

Enhanced IP protection frameworks — particularly important for technology, pharmaceutical, and creative industry exporters.

🔬

Regulatory Cooperation

Mechanisms to reduce non-tariff barriers through regulatory cooperation, reducing compliance costs across EU member states.

🤝

Investment Protection

Investor protection mechanisms providing Canadian businesses with legal certainty when establishing operations across the EEA.

The EICBI Difference

What CETA Cannot Provide — But EICBI Can

CETA provides a legal framework. But relationships open doors. EICBI's 13 years of institutional engagement with EU bodies — and its unique position as a European Parliament-accredited organisation on the EU Transparency Register — provides access that no trade agreement can legislate.

Through CGEC, Canadian businesses gain introductions to Members of the European Parliament, EU Commission officials, and the network of European business leaders and policymakers that shape market conditions across the EEA.
